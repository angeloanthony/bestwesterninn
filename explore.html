✦ Must-See Attractions

Dinosaur Country
Right Outside Your Door

Vernal sits at the heart of one of the most geologically and paleontologically rich regions on earth. Here's what you absolutely can't miss.

🦕 ⭐ Top Attraction ~20 min

National Monument

Dinosaur National Monument

The crown jewel of Vernal. The Quarry Exhibit Hall puts you face-to-face with 1,500+ dinosaur bones still embedded in the cliff face — exactly where they were discovered. Add in canyon hikes, petroglyphs, and the Green River and you'll easily spend a full day here.

Plan Your Visit →
🏛️ Free Entry In Town

Museum · State Park

Utah Field House of Natural History

Walk through 200 million years of geologic time on the famous ramp exhibit. Life-sized dinosaur replicas greet you outside. Inside: real fossils, a working fossil prep lab you can watch through glass, and a knowledgeable staff that doubles as an informal visitor center.

Learn More →
🪨 Hidden Gem ~15 min

Historic Site · Petroglyphs

McConkie Ranch Petroglyphs

One of Utah's best-kept secrets. Near-life-sized Fremont culture figures carved into canyon sandstone — asks only a $5 donation. The main trail requires a short climb but rewards you with some of the most stunning ancient rock art in the American West.

Get Directions →
🔥 Scenic Drive ~40 min

National Recreation Area

Flaming Gorge

200,000+ acres of stunning red canyon, emerald reservoir, and world-class fishing. Boat, waterski, camp, hike, or simply drive the Flaming Gorge–Uintas National Scenic Byway. The dam tour is surprisingly fascinating. Often called "the crown of the Uinta Mountains."

Explore Flaming Gorge →
🦶 Easy Hike ~10 min

State Park · Hiking

Red Fleet State Park

Known as "Little Lake Powell," Red Fleet offers a stunning sandstone lake setting. Hike the Dinosaur Trackway — a 3-mile round trip across sandstone formations to real 200-million-year-old dinosaur footprints, ending at a beautiful swimming spot on the reservoir. Kayak rentals available.

Park Info →
🌀 Wow Factor ~1 hr

Scenic Wonder

Fantasy Canyon

One of the most bizarre and beautiful places in Utah — otherworldly sandstone formations that look like they belong on another planet. About an hour south of Vernal, accessible by passenger car. Visit near sunset for extraordinary photography. Small but absolutely unforgettable.

Directions →
🌉 Easy Trail ~8 min

Hiking · Nature

Moonshine Arch

A stunning 40-foot-tall, 85-foot-wide sandstone arch just 8 miles from town — easy 2-mile round-trip hike with views of Steinaker Reservoir. Shaded caverns behind the arch are perfect for a picnic. Moab doesn't have a monopoly on Utah arches — this one is spectacular and never crowded.

Trail Info →
🐟 Family Favorite ~30 min

Nature · Hiking

Jones Hole Fish Hatchery

A beautiful drive leads to a working fish hatchery where you can watch thousands of trout up close. From here, the Jones Hole Trail follows a stunning creek through canyon walls to Ely Creek Waterfall — one of the most rewarding hikes in the region. Kids love the fish.

✦ Get Outside

More Than Dinosaurs —
Vernal is an Outdoor Paradise

Over 100 miles of mountain biking trails, whitewater on the Green River, kayaking, paddleboarding, ATV tracks, and hiking that rivals anything in Utah. All of it within an hour of your pillow.

🚣 Thrilling ~20 min

Rafting · Adventure

Green River Rafting

Raft through the breathtaking canyons of Dinosaur National Monument on the legendary Green River. Single-day and multi-day trips available. Dinosaur River Expeditions is Vernal's only locally owned outfitter (since 1979). OARS and Adrift Adventures also offer excellent guided trips.

★★★★★
Book a Trip →
🚵 100+ Miles Various

Mountain Biking

McCoy Flats & Trail Network

Vernal's mountain biking is gaining a devoted following — and for good reason. 40+ miles at McCoy Flats alone, plus the Red Fleet Complex, Lapoint Trail System, and the aspen-lined Dry Fork Flume Trail. All without the Moab crowds. Stop at Altitude Cycle on Main Street for maps and advice.

Trail Info →
🚤 Water Fun ~10 min

State Park · Water

Steinaker State Park

Swimming, wakeboarding, kayaking, ATV trails, and camping — all centered around a beautiful reservoir just minutes from town. One of three state parks in the Vernal area. Great for families with kids who need to burn energy after a day of museum-going.

Park Details →
🏍️ Year-Round ~5 min

ATV · Multi-Sport

Buckskin Hills Recreation Complex

Vernal's year-round outdoor sports hub. Mountain biking, ATV trails, motocross, shooting range, drag strip, speedway, camping — and in winter, a ski and snow tubing hill. There is genuinely something for everyone here, in every season.

Learn More →
🎣 Fishing ~40 min

Fishing · Recreation

Flaming Gorge Fishing

Widely considered some of the best fishing in the western United States — record-breaking lake trout, rainbow and brown trout, smallmouth bass, and more in the reservoir and Green River tailwaters. Guided trips available. Bring a license and come prepared to be amazed.

Fishing Info →
⛳ 18 Holes ~5 min

Golf

Dinaland Golf Course

Vernal's local 18-hole golf course set against stunning high-desert scenery. Consistently well-maintained and very reasonably priced. A perfect way to spend a morning before heading out to explore the canyon country. Just minutes from Best Western.

★★★★★

Vernal's crown jewel of dining. The chef trained at a top New York pizza institution and it shows — housemade mozzarella, wood-fired crust with the perfect char, handmade pasta, and a balsamic dressing made in-house. Consistently ranked the #1 restaurant in Vernal. The pistachio pizza is legendary.
