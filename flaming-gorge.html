Why Stay in Vernal for Flaming Gorge?

Lodging options at Flaming Gorge itself are very limited — a handful of small lodges that book up fast, especially in summer. Vernal gives you everything you don't get at the gorge: restaurant options, grocery stores, reliable phone signal, and a full-service hotel with real amenities.

From our front door, you can be launching a boat on the reservoir in under an hour. It's the best of both worlds — a great night's sleep and world-class outdoor recreation right down the road.

What to Do at Flaming Gorge

Flaming Gorge National Recreation Area spans 201,000 acres across northeastern Utah and southwestern Wyoming. Most visitors are surprised by just how much there is to do beyond the famous views.

  • World-class fishing — lake trout, rainbow & brown trout, smallmouth bass
  • Boating, waterskiing & wakeboarding on the reservoir
  • Flaming Gorge–Uintas National Scenic Byway drive
  • Flaming Gorge Dam tour (surprisingly fascinating)
  • Kayaking & paddleboarding in canyon coves
  • Hiking trails through red sandstone canyon country
  • ATV & off-road trails in the Ashley National Forest
  • Dark sky stargazing — among the best in the region
  • Camping and picnicking at multiple developed sites
  • Wildlife viewing — pronghorn, mule deer, eagles

Fishing at Flaming Gorge

Flaming Gorge is widely regarded as one of the best fisheries in the western United States. The reservoir holds world-record lake trout, and the Green River tailwaters below the dam are legendary for brown and rainbow trout on a fly rod.

Pro tip: The Green River below the dam runs cold year-round — excellent fly fishing even in summer when the reservoir is crowded with boaters. Several local guide services operate out of Vernal and the Dutch John area. Ask our front desk for recommendations.

  • Lake trout — record fish over 50 lbs caught here
  • Rainbow & brown trout in the Green River tailwaters
  • Smallmouth bass fishing in warmer months
  • Kokanee salmon in fall
  • Utah fishing license required — available in Vernal
  • Guided trips available from local outfitters

Best Times to Visit Flaming Gorge

🌸 Spring

Excellent fishing, wildflowers, fewer crowds. Snow possible at elevation. Great for hiking.

☀️ Summer

Peak season — boating, water sports, camping. Book early. Very busy July 4th weekend.

🍂 Fall

Best overall — stunning foliage, excellent fishing, cooler temps, manageable crowds.

❄️ Winter

Ice fishing, snowmobiling, solitude. Some facilities close. Call ahead for conditions.

Tips for Your Flaming Gorge Trip

America the Beautiful pass covers the day use fee at Flaming Gorge. If you're also visiting Dinosaur National Monument on the same trip — which is only 20 minutes from Vernal — the pass pays for itself immediately.

  • Book marina rentals in advance for summer weekends
  • Cell service is spotty in canyon areas — download offline maps
  • Afternoon thunderstorms common in summer — plan accordingly
  • The scenic byway drive is worth doing even without stopping
  • Dutch John has a small store but stock up in Vernal before you go
  • Check fs.usda.gov/ashley for current conditions and closures
