Location: Heart of Vernal, Utah

Vernal, Utah (population ~10,000) sits in the Uintah Basin at an elevation of about 5,300 feet. It's the largest city in the region and the main hub for everything in northeastern Utah. You'll find grocery stores, restaurants, fuel, and services all within a short drive of the hotel.

From Vernal you can reach every major attraction in the region without burning your whole day on the drive:

🦕 Dinosaur National Monument

~20 minutes. World-famous fossil quarry, canyon scenic drives, river corridors, and petroglyphs.

🎣 Flaming Gorge

~40 minutes. World-class fishing, boating, Red Canyon overlooks, and the scenic Flaming Gorge Dam.

🏊 Red Fleet State Park

~15 minutes. Swimming, kayaking, dinosaur trackway, and beautiful red-rock reservoir scenery.

🪨 Steinaker State Park

~10 minutes. Water skiing, fishing, and camping on a quiet reservoir just north of town.

🏛️ Utah Field House

~5 minutes. Dinosaur museum right in downtown Vernal — great for families and rainy days.

⛰️ Uinta Mountains

~1 hour. High-altitude hiking, fishing, and camping in one of the nation's great wilderness areas.

About Vernal, Utah

Vernal calls itself "Dinosaurland" for good reason — the surrounding terrain is one of the richest Jurassic-era fossil regions on the planet. But dinosaurs are just the beginning. The Uintah Basin is ringed by canyon lands, river corridors, and mountain wilderness, making it a serious destination for outdoor recreation year-round.

The town itself is friendly and unpretentious — a genuine Utah small city where the people know their neighbors, the restaurants serve real food, and the pace of life gives you room to breathe. If you haven't been to northeastern Utah before, plan to stay longer than you think you'll need.

Local tip: Stop by the Utah Field House of Natural History on Main Street before heading to Dinosaur National Monument — it provides great context for what you'll see at the quarry, and it's right in downtown Vernal.

Getting to Vernal

Vernal is located in northeastern Utah, approximately 170 miles east of Salt Lake City via US-40. It's also accessible from Colorado via US-40 through Dinosaur, CO — a popular route for visitors approaching from Denver or the Front Range.

  • ~2.5 hours from Salt Lake City via US-40
  • ~4 hours from Denver via I-70 and US-40
  • ~45 minutes from the Wyoming border
  • Vernal Regional Airport (VEL) serves regional flights
  • Ample parking for trucks, trailers, and RV tow vehicles
